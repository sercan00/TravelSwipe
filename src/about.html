About TravelSwipe

TravelSwipe is a web application that makes travel planning simple and enjoyable. Instead of reading through lengthy reviews and comparing dozens of options, users discover attractions by swiping through photos — swipe right to save, swipe left to skip.

Liked attractions are collected into a personal itinerary, displayed on an interactive map with optimised walking or driving routes. Users can plan multi-day trips, set a starting point, and filter attractions by category.

How It Works

Select a city, choose how many days your trip will be, and start swiping. Each card shows a photo and short description of an attraction. Swipe right to add it to your itinerary, or left to skip. When you're ready, open the map to see your route planned across your chosen number of days.

About the Project

TravelSwipe was developed by Sercan Emiroglu as a final year BSc Computer Science project at City St George's, University of London (2025–2026), under the supervision of Martin Walter. The project demonstrates frontend interaction design, client-side state management, geospatial routing, and cloud-based session storage.

Technologies Used

  • Leaflet.js — Interactive map rendering with OpenStreetMap tiles
  • OSRM — Open Source Routing Machine for walking and driving routes
  • Firebase Firestore — Cloud-based anonymous session storage
  • HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript (ES6+) — Core frontend technologies
